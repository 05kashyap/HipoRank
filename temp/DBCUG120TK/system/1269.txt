The failure of the respondent to obtain the occupation certificate is a deficiency in service for which the respondent is liable.
In the present case the respondent was responsible for transferring the title to the flats to the society along with the occupancy certificate.
Thus the members of the appellant society are well within their rights as ‘consumers’ to pray for compensation as a recompense for the consequent liabilityarising from the lack of an occupancy certificate.
